Vector search finds similarity by measuring distance between embeddings.
Cosine similarity measures the angle between two vectors in high-dimensional space.
A similarity of 1.0 means identical meaning. A similarity of 0.0 means unrelated.
Traditional databases use exact keyword matching which fails for natural language.
Vector databases like ChromaDB are optimized for nearest-neighbor search.
The embedding model must be the same at ingest time and query time.
Chunk size affects retrieval precision — smaller chunks are more focused.
TOP_K controls how many chunks are returned per query.
