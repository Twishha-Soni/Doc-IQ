RAG stands for Retrieval-Augmented Generation.
It solves the problem of AI models answering from stale training data.
The pattern has three steps: ingest, retrieve, and generate.
Ingestion breaks documents into chunks and converts them to vector embeddings.
Retrieval finds the most semantically similar chunks for a given query.
Generation uses the retrieved chunks as context to produce a grounded answer.
RAG prevents hallucination by anchoring answers in real documents.
The quality of chunking and embedding directly affects answer quality.
